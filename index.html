What is Starlink jitter?

Jitter is the variation in your ping — and it's the real reason Starlink gaming feels broken.

Satellite handoffs

Starlink continuously switches between satellites in low Earth orbit. Each handoff causes a brief coverage gap — your games see this as a 200–400ms spike every 45 seconds.

Why ping alone doesn't matter

A 40ms average ping sounds great. But if it spikes to 400ms for half a second during every handoff, you're rubber-banding and disconnecting regardless.

Rain fade & obstruction

Bad weather and trees cause additional outages on top of handoffs. Each outage shows up as a spike in our test — and a disconnect in your game.

The fix: path bonding

AntiJitter bonds Starlink with 4G/5G simultaneously. When Starlink drops, 4G covers the gap in milliseconds. Zero spikes. Zero disconnects.
